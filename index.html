Dear Maddie,

At the start of summer I was laid off again so I decided to go all in on the music stuff. I used all the money I had saved to work with a guy I thought would be a long term partner. I paid him to help me record what was meant to be my debut EP, doing the wrong thing. But he took my money and stole my files. I trusted him blindly despite some red flags and paid the price. It's silly but I thought contracts would muddy the waters. To make matters worse my hard drive died so I lost all the music I was working on.

Still I gave it my all and that is exactly what I set out to do. But I lost myself and it literally cost me everything. I was so obsessed I let my relationships, mental and physical health slip. Anyway, I learned some valuable lessons 1) how you get there is what you get there, 2) if there’s no love it’s no good and 3) don’t be so impulsive. I don't really have anywhere to go but I'm sure when I get there I'll know. I've got my guitar, a few empty notebooks and lots of time. Figure I'll hit the road, no expectations, just go with the flow. Whatever happens, happens.

Jeter
